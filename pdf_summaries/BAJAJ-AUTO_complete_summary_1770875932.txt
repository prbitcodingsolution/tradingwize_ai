**Financial Summary**

**Financial Performance**
- Bajaj Auto Limited reported resilient financial performance in Q1 FY26 with revenue from operations at ₹12,584 crores, a 6% increase year-over-year (YoY).
- The company's EBITDA stood at ₹2,482 crores with a margin of 19.7%, down 50 basis points (bps) quarter-over-quarter (QoQ) primarily due to lower dollar realization.
- Profit after tax for the quarter was ₹2,096 crores, a 5% increase YoY, showcasing strong profitability.

**Business Highlights**
- Bajaj Auto witnessed double-digit growth in premium motorcycles, commercial vehicles (CVs), and Chetak, driving the highest-ever Q1 revenue in the domestic market.
- The company's electric vehicles now constitute over 20% of domestic revenues, reflecting a strategic shift towards electrification.
- Quarterly export revenues reached a historic peak, supported by double-digit growth across key regions like Africa, Latin America, and Asia.
- Domestic motorcycle sales grew double digits, led by the 125cc+ segment, which saw market share gains and impactful activations.
- Commercial Vehicles achieved over 100,000 quarterly retails for the 8th consecutive quarter, with electric 3Ws becoming a significant driver of growth.
- Chetak's retail volumes more than doubled YoY, contributing significantly to incremental volume in the industry.

**Financial Health**
- The balance sheet of Bajaj Auto remains healthy with a sustained track record of free cash flow generation, adding approximately ₹1,200 crores in the quarter.
- Surplus funds stood at ₹16,726 crores, with investments made in Bajaj Auto Credit and Bajaj Auto International Holdings BV to support growth initiatives.

**Future Outlook**
- Bajaj Auto aims to continue its focus on electrification, premiumization, and exports to drive growth in the upcoming quarters.
- The company plans to further strengthen its position in the electric vehicle segment and capitalize on the growing demand for premium motorcycles.
- Risks related to geopolitical challenges in certain export markets and supply disruptions due to raw material shortages need to be managed effectively.
- Opportunities lie in expanding market share in the domestic and international markets, especially in the premium motorcycle and electric vehicle segments.

In conclusion, Bajaj Auto's Q1 FY26 results reflect a robust financial performance driven by strategic initiatives in electrification, premiumization, and export expansion. The company's strong profitability, focus on innovation, and prudent financial management position it well for future growth despite existing challenges in the global automotive industry.
