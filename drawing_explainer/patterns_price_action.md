Patterns in trading are recurring, identifiable shapes formed by price movements on a chart, used to predict future price direction based on historical trends. They are a core component of technical analysis, helping traders identify support/resistance, market psychology, and potential trend reversals or continuations.Key Aspects of Trading Patterns:Purpose: Patterns help traders understand the battle between buyers and sellers to predict future price moves, such as 10 chart patterns every trader needs to know (IG) or 11 Trading Chart Patterns You Should Know (FOREX.com).Types of Patterns:Continuation: Signals that an existing trend will continue (e.g., flags, wedges).Reversal: Signals that an existing trend is likely to change direction (e.g., Head and Shoulders, Double Top/Bottom).Bilateral: Signals the price could break out in either direction (e.g., Symmetrical Triangle).Common Examples: Head and shoulders, 38 Candlestick Patterns for Pro Traders (Groww), double tops/bottoms, triangles, and cup-and-handle formations.Analysis Type Used: Technical AnalysisTrading patterns are almost exclusively used within Technical Analysis, which focuses on analyzing statistical trends gathered from trading activity, such as price movement and volume.Chartists & Analysts: Use, as defined in this Investopedia article, patterns to determine entry and exit points.Based on Human Behavior: Patterns rely on the premise that investor psychology repeats, creating predictable, repeatable price formations.Supported by Tools: Analysts may also use 14 Chart Patterns Traders Should Know (Trade Nation) as indicators to confirm trends, alongside volume analysis.

Price action refers to the movement of a security's price over time, plotted on charts to analyze market behavior and predict future price movements without relying on lagging indicators. It is a form of technical analysis that uses raw price data, such as candlestick charts, support/resistance levels, trendlines, and chart patterns, often described as "clean chart" or "naked" trading.Key Components and Analysis Types Used:Candlestick Patterns: Traders analyze specific shapes (e.g., Doji, Hammer, Engulfing) to understand market psychology.Support and Resistance Levels: Identifying horizontal areas where prices previously reversed, acting as floors or ceilings.Trendlines and Channels: Drawing lines to determine the direction of the market (uptrend or downtrend).Chart Patterns: Identifying structures like Head and Shoulders, Triangles, or Flags to predict potential breakouts.Price action is considered a pure form of technical analysis, focusing on the "territory" (actual price) rather than the "map" (indicators). It is used to identify entry and exit points for short to medium-term trading.
